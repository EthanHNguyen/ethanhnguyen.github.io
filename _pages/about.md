# AI/ML Engineer @ Google

**Scaling Gemini to the edge.** I build frontier AI systems that move from research-grade capability into reliable, production-grade infrastructure.

My work sits at the intersection of machine learning systems, distributed infrastructure, and applied AI: making models faster, more dependable, and easier to deploy where real users and real constraints live.

Before Google, I built large-scale ML and cloud platforms at Capital One. I helped design and develop an internal Jupyter Notebook-based platform used by thousands of data scientists, machine learning engineers, and analysts to train models for banking use cases including fraud detection, customer segmentation, and risk management. Earlier, I helped build credit application infrastructure for new card launches and migrated high-transaction microservices to AWS Fargate to improve reliability and operating cost.

My research background is in deep learning, computer vision, and medical image analysis. At Vanderbilt University, I developed new ML algorithms, published peer-reviewed research, and built the foundation for the systems-oriented approach I use today: strong models matter, but deployment, observability, data quality, and human trust determine whether they actually create value.

I’m interested in frontier AI infrastructure, edge deployment, model evaluation, agentic systems, and the practical engineering required to bring advanced AI into high-stakes environments.
