Selected public builds at the edge of frontier AI, high-trust deployment, and public-data interfaces. The common thread is simple: turn messy institutional workflows into systems people can actually use.

Featured Builds

ATO Copilot

Source-backed AI for government authorization workflows. ATO Copilot is a hackathon prototype that turns synthetic evidence artifacts into structured control analysis, reviewer questions, recommended actions, and provenance traces.

  • What it proves: high-trust AI systems should be workflow-native, evidence-backed, and designed to preserve human judgment.
  • Links: Project page · Writeup · Source

RFP Map

Mobile-first market intelligence radar for SAM.gov opportunities. RFP Map turns federal contract opportunities into a spatial browsing interface: explore agencies, themes, opportunity clusters, and source-linked contract pages without needing to understand procurement search syntax.

  • What it proves: public procurement data can become market terrain: something to explore, cluster, and reason over, not just search.
  • Links: Project page · Live · Source
